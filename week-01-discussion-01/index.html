Discussion 01: Student Introductions & The Purpose of a Personal Site

Part 01: Hello everyone! My name is Marisol, and I’m from Paradise, Texas. I’m currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computing Applications with a minor in Anthropology at Texas Tech University, and I plan to graduate in Spring 2027. I stepped away from school for several years during COVID and found a career in the insurance industry, but I recently returned to finish my degree.

Outside of academics, I enjoy reading and traveling, especially anything that involves exploring new places or theme parks. I shared a photo of my family standing in front of Super Nintendo World at Universal Studios during a trip we took to celebrate my brother’s high school graduation. It was my first time visiting, and it made the experience even more memorable because I got to share it with family. A fun fact about me is that I really enjoy planning trips and creating detailed itineraries almost as much as the travel itself.

Part 02: In today’s world of social media, AI-generated content, and short videos, having a personal website is valuable because it gives me a space that I fully control. It allows me to present my work, skills, and growth in a professional way without relying on platforms that limit how content is shown. For me as a beginner in computing, it also serves as something I can build over time as I gain more experience, which will be helpful when applying for real jobs after college.

Learning HTML and CSS is important because it helps me understand how websites are actually built instead of just using templates or automated tools. Even though those tools are convenient, learning the code gives me a stronger foundation, better problem-solving skills, and more confidence to create or fix things on my own in the future.
