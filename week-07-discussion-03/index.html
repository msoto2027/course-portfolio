Discussion 03: The Living Web

After working on my Course Portfolio, Course Homepage, and Personal Landing Page over the past few weeks, I understand Laurel Schwulst's essay My website is a shifting house... (Schwulst, 2018) a lot better. At first, I thought a website had to be perfect before anyone could see it. Now I see that it's okay for it to change and grow over time. Every time I updated my pages or added something new, it felt more like a reflection of my progress instead of something that had to be completely finished. I also enjoyed adding my own personal touch through the colors, layout, and content, which made the website feel more like it represents me. That idea makes building a website feel a lot less stressful.

For my Professional Portfolio, I want it to show who I am and what I've learned so far. I plan to include my best projects, highlight the skills I've developed, and let my personality come through in the design. I hope it will be useful when applying for internships or jobs because it gives employers a chance to see both my work and who I am. It's something I can continue updating as I gain more experience and build new skills.

Reference:

Schwulst, L. (2018, May 21). My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be? The Creative Independent. https://thecreativeindependent.com/essays/laurel-schwulst-my-website-is-a-shifting-house-next-to-a-river-of-knowledge-what-could-yours-be/
