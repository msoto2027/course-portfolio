1. Coachella

I like this website because it creates a strong visual identity that matches the energy and creativity of the festival. The use of bold images, colors, and typography makes the website feel like an extension of the event itself. It inspires me to think about how a website can create a mood and experience rather than just provide information.

Visit Coachella

2. Reactor

I like this website because it focuses on fantasy and science fiction books while creating a strong sense of community for readers. The site includes articles, book discussions, and content about fantasy stories that have been adapted into movies and TV shows, such as House of the Dragon. I like how it connects readers with both the original books and their screen adaptations. It inspires me to think about how a blog can combine storytelling, design, and community to create an engaging experience for visitors.

Visit Reactor

3. Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History

I like this website because it does a great job organizing a large amount of information while still keeping the design visually interesting. The use of images, categories, and interactive features makes it easy for visitors to explore exhibits and learn about different topics. It inspires me to think about how a website can educate users while creating an engaging and enjoyable experience.

Visit Smithsonian
